Cursorfly - Screen Recorder with Auto Pan Zoom

Record your browser in three simple steps

1

Share this screen

Make sure to select the screen that is displaying this browser window. For best results, select the "Entire screen" option when prompted.

🔊 Audio Recording:

IMPORTANT: When the sharing dialog appears, you MUST check the checkbox for "Share tab audio" or "Share system audio" to record audio. If you don't check this box, your recording will be video-only.

Note: Turning on system audio in the share dialog will result in microphone audio not working for that recording. Use tab audio only if you need both screen sound and your mic, or skip system audio and use Enable microphone in step 2 instead.

Screen selected - Ready to record
2

Camera & microphone (optional)

Choose what to add — each button asks for one permission only. Use Camera for a picture-in-picture overlay on the recording tab. Use Microphone to mix your voice with screen audio. You can enable one, both, or neither. If you turned on system audio in step 1, mic recording may not work.

3

Hide screen sharing bar

Unless hidden, the bar at the bottom will appear in your recording. If the bar appears on another screen, you can simply ignore it.

4

Start recording

You will be taken to your previous tab and recording will start immediately. Stop the recording by pressing the extension's button in the toolbar or from this pinned tab.

💡 Bonus tip: To start and stop recording easily, pin Cursorfly - Screen Recorder with Auto Pan Zoom to your extensions toolbar.
